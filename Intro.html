Life

To Get Something You Never Had, You Have To Do Something You Never Did



It's difficult to find meaning.



In a world full of conflict and despair many people question this game of life. But your situation isn't exactly unique, around 117 Billion of us humans have played the game of life, some of which have asked themselves the same questions you have. Some of which struggled to find the purpose of it all. That's a rather terrifying thought. Although life, actually, is pretty unique. The chances of you being here, right now, reading this are low. In fact, the proability of you just existing is around one in ten (followed by two million six-hundred and eighty five thousand zeroes). I'd say that's pretty absurd and you may be inclined to feel the same way too. Nevertheless, being aware of these odds still doesn't really answer the true question of meaning. And it doesn't have to. The odds of you being here don't prove anything other than how absurd it is. But what if that is the meaning? To take note of the absurd and find meaning in it. To embrace the absurdity that surrounds you and accept it for how unfathomable it truly is. To feel things. To embrace failure, feeling uncomfortable, challenging yourself, doubt it all and question why you ever done it in the first place only to do it all over again. Embrace love, and heartbreak, embrace loss but also discovery. Embrace life. Why? I don't know. But you wouldn't be here reading this now if there wasn't something worth living for.
